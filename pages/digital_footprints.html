DIGITAL FOOTPRINTS

What is a Digital Footprint?

When you use the internet, numerous sites are collecting information about you. Sites will collect information when you sign up for an account, for when you decide to share location information, for when you enable cookies – your personal data is scattered among a variety of websites, and it can be used to identify you. Your digital footprint does not just consist of information you explicitly decide to share, however. It can also include things like what ads you click on and what articles you read. Some examples of data involved in a digital footprint may include:

  • Personal information like your name, email address, or phone number
  • Banking information for making purchases
  • Site and search history
  • Your IP address
  • People you are friends with in social media
  • What times of the day you are most active online

As you can see, digital footprints have a lot of information in them. Even if an attacker does not have what we often view as “personal information”, like your name or your phone number, your data is still personally identifiable in other ways- like by IP address or by connecting an email address to a bank account. Unfortunately, using the internet means that you will have information collected about you. It is not possible to completely erase a digital footprint, but it is possible to take certain safety measures to help protect you as best possible:

  • Limit the data that you make available – do not opt in to share your email address, phone number, location, etc. unless it is necessary for the service you are using.
  • Avoid linking your social media to other websites – when you have different information available on different websites that are linked together, it is essentially like having all of the information available on the same website. Linking accounts makes you more vulnerable during data breaches, so try to keep your accounts confined to their own websites.
  • Update your passwords to be more secure, especially when data breaches are announced. If your information has been compromised, this will help better protect it.

It is important to be aware of your digital footprint, especially in terms of what you post on social media. Your posts on social media can not only be used to identify you, but they also can be seen by people outside your inner circle, you should always think before you post.
