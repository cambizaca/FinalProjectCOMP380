DATA COLLECTION

What type of data is being collected?

  • Personal data: This includes personal information such as one's Social Security numbers, gender, etc. This also includes information such as your IP address, web browser cookies and device IDs.
  • Engagement data: This consists of how consumers interact with a business's website, mobile app, text messages, social media pages, emails, paid ads and customer service routes.
  • Behavioral data: This data is on transactional details such as purchase history, product usage information (ex. a consumer's repeated action) and qualitative data (ex. information on mouse movement).
  • Attitudinal data: This data includes metrics on consumer satisfaction, purchase criteria, product desirability and much more.

How is this data being collected?

Data can be collected from many sources. Some are more technical while others are more deductive. Companies have their own methods and sources when it comes to collecting and processing customer data on metrics. Businesses collect data in 3 ways:

  • Directly asking customers.
  • Indirectly tracking customers
  • Appending other sources of customer data to your own.

The way most social media platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and other big companies collect data from consumers includes:

  • Through a consumers device
  • Google searches
  • Things one likes, posts or accepts on their social media account

These companies and data scientists collect our data to build personas about us, consumers, to determine our age, gender and what we like more, etc. (this means they create computer-based personalities) thus they can include ads of brands we search more often, know possible people who are close friends and place them in our suggested friends list to add, and can even use the location services data (if granted permission by consumer) to provide stores, friends and more near you.

What do these social media companies use this data for?

  • This data can be used to improve customer experience to gain a better understanding and meet their consumer's demands.
  • This gives the company the knowledge to modify their digital presence, goods, or services to better suit the consumer.
  • The data helps with marketing strategies for these companies to know what consumers want.
  • Data can also be turned into cash flow thus companies profit from the data being collected.
