DATA BREACHES

How secure is your data?

Given that so much data is collected by social media companies, it it important to understand how secure that data is and what happens when there are data breaches. Even though a lot of social media companies have had at least one major data privacy scandal, their privacy policies are not very descriptive about how data is kept secure. Take a look at the following quotes from different privacy policies:

“We use this information for things like keeping your account secure and showing you more relevant tweets, people to follow, events, and ads.”

“We use the information we have to verify accounts and activity… and promote safety and security on and off of Facebook products.”

“We implement security safeguards designed to protect your data, such as HTTPS. We regularly monitor our systems for possible vulnerabilities and attacks.”

What company makes you feel the best about the security of your data?

Of these three social media sites, only LinkedIn specifically stated that the data collected by them was kept secure and that they consistently monitored it to keep it that way. Facebook and Twitter, on the other hand, admit that they collect data, and they do say that they use it to keep your account secure, but they don’t say anything about what would happen if there was some type of data breach. Other social media sites, such as Instagram, don’t even make any references to data privacy in their privacy policy. Although all of these companies have had significant privacy breaches, only one of them talks about preventing them in the future. Interestingly, these companies all claim to have highly secure data when their level of data security is questioned, usually following a data breach, but they rarely claim to have secure data in times when they are not under the spotlight.

Case Study: Facebook Data Breach

In a relatively recent data breach, over 530 million Facebook users had their personal data, including phone numbers, scraped from a vulnerability. The vulnerability came from a feature that had been removed from Facebook, but was still accessible by hackers. This feature allowed users to find each other by their phone number, meaning that the hackers were able to access user’s phone numbers, which is often a personally identifiable piece of information. After this data was accessed, it was then posted on a hacking forum and was thus publicly available.

The most interesting part about this data breach, however, is Facebook’s response. In an internal email which was leaked to a news source, it was revealed that they would try to frame the data breach as a “normal occurrence” and would say that the data came from “publicly available information”. They said that they did not plan to inform users about the data breach, instead favoring their own reputation. Although the breached data did not include password information, it is still necessary to tell users that their phone numbers have been leaked, as they can be connected to accounts on other websites and can still be used to pinpoint one’s identity. Facebook’s response to its data breach shows a lack of integrity and raises questions of what other data breaches may have happened that were simply swept under the rug.
